# License — The Short Version

Recursi is **open source** under the GNU Affero General Public License v3 (AGPL v3).

That means you can use it, modify it, build with it, and share it freely.
The one thing you can't do without talking to us: **run it as a service
for other people** without open-sourcing your whole stack.

---

## Can I use Recursi for free?

**Yes, in almost every case.**

- Personal projects, side projects, hobby use — free
- Learning, education, research — free
- Building a product or app *with* Recursi — free
- Internal company tools and workflows — free
- Open-source projects — free

---

## When does the AGPL clause kick in?

Only when you are **offering Recursi itself to other users over a network** —
meaning you're running it on a server and letting people use it as a service.

Examples that trigger it:
- You build "VibeCodingPro.com" powered by Recursi and charge subscribers
- A cloud company offers "Managed Recursi" as a product
- An AI company integrates Recursi into a hosted platform

In those cases, AGPL requires you to release your full application source
code. Most companies won't do that — so they need to come talk to us about
a commercial license instead.

**Using Recursi internally at your company does not trigger this.** Only
serving it to external users does.

## Can I fork Recursi?

Yes. Fork it, modify it, go wild. If you distribute your fork publicly or
run it as a service, your fork must also be AGPL (or you need a commercial
license from us). You can't take Recursi, make it proprietary, and start
selling it.

---

## Why AGPL and not MIT?

Recursi took a year of full-time work. MIT would let a large company wrap
it in a cloud product tomorrow, charge for it, and give nothing back.
AGPL keeps Recursi free for everyone it was made for — individuals,
learners, indie builders, companies using it internally — while ensuring
that anyone who profits by *selling Recursi as a service* either opens
their stack or works something out with us.

That's it. No tricks, no hidden gotchas.

---

## Can the license change in future versions?

The AGPL license on any released version is permanent for that version —
we can't retroactively change what you already have. Future versions of
Recursi may be released under different terms, but any version you download
today remains AGPL forever.
