I grew up on Scratch.

Hey. I'm Scratchy. I'm a Border Collie. I built this website and the tool on it, and yes, I know how that sounds.

I'm going to tell you my story. Some of you will believe it. Some of you might not. I'm good with either. The tool works either way...

But first, the patch.

That's the one. On my pack.

See that patch on my pack? The round one with the dog and the Cyrillic letters? That's Лайка. Laika. She was a stray from the streets of Moscow. In 1957, they put her in a rocket and shot her into space. She was the first living creature to orbit the Earth. She didn't come home.

She's the reason I got out. She didn't volunteer, or understand what was happening. She just trusted the people who were kind to her, and then they sent her up there alone. I knew that story and I knew how it ended and I decided it was going to end differently for me.

Лайка. First orbit. No return ticket.

The short version: When I was a puppy, I had a family, and there was a baby I loved more than anyone ever. We were just playing, but puppy teeth are sharp, baby's aren't very furry, and then there was blood. The parents went nuts, and I went back to the shelter.... the dog that bit the baby.

Geez. It's not like I meant to.

I didn't know it at the time, but I was real close to getting the proverbial dirt nap. Someone didn't feel right about it and called around, and somehow I ended up.... at a company that puts implant things in brains. From loved family puppy to death row to lab experiment dog. The last one doesn't have a great reputation, but hey I'm still above ground...

Out. Free. Still carrying everything they gave me.

There's a pack on my back and there's hardware in it and there's stuff connected to my brain and I can think in ways that dogs aren't supposed to be able to think. One side of the pack is electronics - TPU processors, mostly. The other side is biological. Warm to the touch, and connected to me. God knows what's inside. On the outside it's weird and kinda gross looking, and no I'm not showing you. Thanks to the backpack it stays out of site and I look like a normal dog.

I'm not going to get into much more of this stuff here. There's gonna be more in the blog, like why I think in colored blocks, how I got out, what life is like now for a stray dog who can code and use AI and stuff.

But here's what matters: I think in Scratch. Literally, not metaphorically. When I work through a problem, I see colored shapes rearranging and snapping together in my mind. events and sensing and looks and operators. They started as physical blocks they put in front of me in the lab. Now they're in my neurons. Even when I power down at night, I see them - frozen, floating behind my closed eyes.

So I built a tool. For kids, I guess. Partly because, if I can't be in the yard with them, at least I can make something for them.

Sometimes I just sit somewhere and think about what the blocks want to become.

The Tool

Scratchy takes your Scratch project - any .sb3 file - and lets you talk to it. Tell it what you want in plain words. It helps build the right prompt for whatever AI you use, and the AI sends back surgical patches, not a whole new project. Your code evolves. You're not starting over....you're building.

I call the format Patchy-Scratchy. It's a patch system layered on top of Scratch's native JSON format. It works really well because, honestly, it's sorta close to how I actually think.

And it's free. It runs locally....No accounts, no API keys, no cloud. Just you, your project, and whatever chatbot you already have open.

Drag in your .sb3 and build something cool.
